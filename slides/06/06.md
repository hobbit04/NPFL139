# Policy Gradient Methods

Instead of predicting expected returns, we could train a method to directly
predict the policy
$$ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё).$$

~~~
Obtaining the full distribution over all actions would also allow us to sample
the actions according to the distribution $ПҖ$ instead of just $Оө$-greedy
sampling.

~~~
However, to train the network, we maximize the expected return $v_ПҖ(s)$, and to
that account we need to compute its _gradient_ $вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s)$.

---
# Policy Gradient Methods

In addition to discarding $Оө$-greedy action selection, policy gradient methods
allow producing policies which are by nature **stochastic**, as in multi-player
games like rock paper scissors, card games with imperfect information, or in
partially-observable environments, while the action-value methods have no natural way of
finding stochastic policies (distributional RL might be of some use though).

~~~
![w=68.5%,f=right](stochastic_policy_example.svgz)

In the example, the reward is -1 per step, and we assume the three states appear
identical under the function approximation.

---
# Policy Gradient Theorem

Let $ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё)$ be a parametrized policy. We denote the initial state
distribution as $h(s)$ and the on-policy distribution under $ПҖ$ as $Ој(s)$.
Let also $J(вҶ’Оё) вүқ рқ”ј_{sвҲјh} v_ПҖ(s)$.

~~~
Then
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s) вҲқ вҲ‘_{s'вҲҲрқ“ў} P(s вҶ’ вҖҰ вҶ’ s'|ПҖ) вҲ‘_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} q_ПҖ(s', a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s'; вҶ’Оё)$$
~~~
and
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} J(вҶ’Оё) вҲқ вҲ‘_{sвҲҲрқ“ў} Ој(s) вҲ‘_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё),$$

~~~
where $P(s вҶ’ вҖҰ вҶ’ s'|ПҖ)$ is the probability of being in state $s'$ when
considering trajectories starting in $s$ and following $ПҖ$. The $Оі$ parameter should
be treated as a form of termination, i.e., $P(s вҶ’ вҖҰ вҶ’ s'|ПҖ) вҲқ вҲ‘_{k=0}^вҲһ Оі^k P(s вҶ’ s'\textrm{~in~}k\textrm{~steps~}|ПҖ)$.

---
# Proof of Policy Gradient Theorem

$\displaystyle вҲҮv_ПҖ(s) = вҲҮ \Big[ вҲ‘\nolimits_a ПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) q_ПҖ(s, a) \Big]$

~~~
$\displaystyle \phantom{вҲҮv_ПҖ(s)} = вҲ‘\nolimits_a \Big[ q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ ПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) + ПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ q_ПҖ(s, a) \Big]$

~~~
$\displaystyle \phantom{вҲҮv_ПҖ(s)} = вҲ‘\nolimits_a \Big[ q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ ПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) + ПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) вҲҮ \big(вҲ‘\nolimits_{s', r} p(s', r|s, a)(r + Оіv_ПҖ(s'))\big) \Big]$

~~~
$\displaystyle \phantom{вҲҮv_ПҖ(s)} = вҲ‘\nolimits_a \Big[ q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ ПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) + ОіПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) \big(вҲ‘\nolimits_{s'} p(s'|s, a) вҲҮ v_ПҖ(s')\big) \Big]$

~~~
_We now expand $v_ПҖ(s')$._

~~~
$\displaystyle \phantom{вҲҮv_ПҖ(s)} = вҲ‘\nolimits_a \Big[ q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ ПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) + ОіПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё) \Big(вҲ‘\nolimits_{s'} p(s'|s, a)\Big(\\
                \quad\qquad\qquad вҲ‘\nolimits_{a'} \Big[ q_ПҖ(s', a') вҲҮ ПҖ(a'|s'; вҶ’Оё) + ОіПҖ(a'|s'; вҶ’Оё) \big(вҲ‘\nolimits_{s''} p(s''|s', a') вҲҮ v_ПҖ(s'')\big) \Big] \Big) \Big) \Big]$

~~~
_Continuing to expand all $v_ПҖ(s'')$, we obtain the following:_

$\displaystyle вҲҮv_ПҖ(s) = вҲ‘_{s'вҲҲрқ“ў} вҲ‘_{k=0}^вҲһ Оі^k P(s вҶ’ s'\textrm{~in~}k\textrm{~steps~}|ПҖ) вҲ‘_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} q_ПҖ(s', a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s'; вҶ’Оё).$

---
# Proof of Policy Gradient Theorem

The first part of the proof is therefore finished when we use that
$$вҲ‘_{k=0}^вҲһ Оі^k P(s вҶ’ s'\textrm{~in~}k\textrm{~steps~}|ПҖ) вҲқ P(s вҶ’ вҖҰ вҶ’ s'|ПҖ).$$

~~~
For the second part, we now know that
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} J(вҶ’Оё) = рқ”ј_{s вҲј h} вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} v_ПҖ(s) вҲқ рқ”ј_{s вҲј h} вҲ‘_{s'вҲҲрқ“ў} P(s вҶ’ вҖҰ вҶ’ s'|ПҖ) вҲ‘_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} q_ПҖ(s', a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s'; вҶ’Оё),$$
~~~
therefore using the fact that $Ој(s') = рқ”ј_{s вҲј h} P(s вҶ’ вҖҰ вҶ’ s'|ПҖ)$ we get
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} J(вҶ’Оё) вҲқ вҲ‘_{sвҲҲрқ“ў} Ој(s) вҲ‘_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё).$$

# The REINFORCE Algorithm

The REINFORCE algorithm (Williams, 1992) directly uses the policy gradient
theorem, minimizing $-J(вҶ’Оё) вүқ -рқ”ј_{sвҲјh} v_ПҖ(s)$. The loss gradient is then
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} -J(вҶ’Оё) вҲқ -вҲ‘_{sвҲҲрқ“ў} Ој(s) вҲ‘_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё) = -рқ”ј_{s вҲј Ој} вҲ‘_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё).$$

~~~
However, the sum over all actions is problematic. Instead, we rewrite it to an
expectation, which we can estimate by sampling:
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} -J(вҶ’Оё) вҲқ рқ”ј_{s вҲј Ој} рқ”ј_{a вҲј ПҖ} q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} -\ln ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё),$$
~~~
where we used the fact that
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} \ln ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё) = \frac{1}{ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё)} вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё).$$

---
# The REINFORCE Algorithm

REINFORCE therefore minimizes the loss $-J(вҶ’Оё)$ with gradient
$$рқ”ј_{s вҲј Ој} рқ”ј_{a вҲј ПҖ} q_ПҖ(s, a) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} -\ln ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё),$$
where we estimate the $q_ПҖ(s, a)$ by a single sample.

Note that the loss is just a weighted variant of negative log-likelihood (NLL),
where the sampled actions play the role of gold labels and are weighted according
to their return.

# On-policy Distribution in REINFORCE

In the proof, we assumed $Оі$ is used as a form of termination in the definition
of the on-policy distribution.

~~~
However, even when discounting is used during training (to guarantee convergence
even for very long episodes), evaluation is often performed without discounting.

~~~
Consequently, the distribution $Ој$ used in the REINFORCE algorithm is almost
always the unterminated (undiscounted) on-policy distribution (I am not
aware of any implementation or paper that would use the discounted one), so that
we learn even in states that are far from the beginning of an episode.

~~~
Note that this is actually true even for DQN and its variants. Therefore,
the discounting parameter $Оі$ is used mostly as a variance-reduction technique.

# REINFORCE with Baseline

The returns can be arbitrary: when you obtain a single return of +100,
without further information you cannot distinguish whether it is
better than the average or worse than the average.

~~~
Fortunately, we can generalize the policy gradient theorem using a baseline $b(s)$
to
$$вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} J(вҶ’Оё) вҲқ вҲ‘_{sвҲҲрқ“ў} Ој(s) вҲ‘_{a вҲҲ рқ“җ} \big(q_ПҖ(s, a) - \boldsymbol{b(s)}\big) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё).$$

~~~
The baseline $b(s)$ can be a function or even a random variable, as long as it
does not depend on $a$, because
$$вҲ‘_a b(s) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё) = b(s) вҲ‘_a вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё) = b(s) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} вҲ‘_a ПҖ(a | s; вҶ’Оё) = b(s) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} 1 = 0.$$

---
# REINFORCE with Baseline

A good choice for $b(s)$ is $v_ПҖ(s)$, which can be shown to minimize the
variance of the gradient estimate (in the limit $Оі вҶ’ 1$; see L. Weaver and N. Tao,
_The Optimal Reward Baseline for Gradient-Based Reinforcement Learning_,
https://arxiv.org/abs/1301.2315, for the proof).
Such baseline reminds centering of returns, given that
$$v_ПҖ(s) = рқ”ј_{a вҲј ПҖ} q_ПҖ(s, a).$$

~~~
Then, better-than-average returns are positive and worse-than-average returns
are negative.

~~~
The resulting $q_ПҖ(s, a) - v_ПҖ(s)$ function is also called the **advantage** function
$$a_ПҖ(s, a) вүқ q_ПҖ(s, a) - v_ПҖ(s).$$

~~~
Of course, the $v_ПҖ(s)$ baseline can be only approximated. If a neural network
is used to estimate $ПҖ(a|s; вҶ’Оё)$, then some part of the network can be
shared between the policy and value function estimation, which is trained as
in value methods, i.e., using the mean squared value (mean squared error of the
predicted and observed return).

---
# REINFORCE with Baseline

In REINFORCE with baseline, we train:
1. the _policy network_ using the REINFORCE algorithm, and
~~~
2. the _value network_ by minimizing the mean squared value error
   $\overline{VE}$.

# Operator View of Policy Gradient Methods

In the middle of 2020, _Dibya Ghosh et al._ introduced the operator view of
policy gradient methods in their paper
_An operator view of policy gradient methods_, https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.11266.

~~~
## Trajectory Formulation

Let $П„ = (S_0, A_0, S_1, A_1, вҖҰ)$ be a specific trajectory with return
$G(П„) = вҲ‘_{k=0}^вҲһ Оі^k R_{k+1}(П„)$.
~~~
The probability of $П„$ under a policy $ПҖ$ is $ПҖ(П„) = h(S_0) вҲҸ_i ПҖ(A_i | S_i)
p(S_{i+1} | S_i, A_i)$.

~~~
Our goal is then to find
$$вҶ’Оё^* = \argmax_{вҶ’Оё} рқ”ј_{П„ вҲј ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}} \big[G(П„)\big] = \argmax_{вҶ’Оё} вҲ«_П„ ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}(П„) G(П„) \d П„,$$
~~~
and the REINFORCE algorithm at each step sets the weights $вҶ’Оё_{t+1}$ to
~~~
$$вҶ’Оё_t + Оұрқ”ј_{П„ вҲј ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё_t}}\bigg[G(П„) \frac{вҲӮ\log ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}(П„)}{вҲӮвҶ’Оё}\Big|_{вҶ’Оё=вҶ’Оё_t} \bigg]
= вҶ’Оё_t + ОұвҲ«_П„ ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё_t}(П„) G(П„) \frac{вҲӮ\log ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}(П„)}{вҲӮвҶ’Оё}\Big|_{вҶ’Оё=вҶ’Оё_t} \d П„.$$

---
# Trajectory Formulation of OP-REINFORCE

In the operator view, policy improvement is achieved by a successive application
of a **policy improvement operator** $рқ“ҳ$ and a **projection operator** $рқ“ҹ$. For
tabular methods, the projection operator is identity, but it is needed for
functional approximation methods.

~~~
The operator version of REINFORCE is then the iterative application of $рқ“ҹвҲҳрқ“ҳ$
with
$$(рқ“ҳ ПҖ)(П„) \stackrel{\tiny\textrm{def}}{вҲқ} G(П„) ПҖ(П„),$$
~~~
$$рқ“ҹ ОҪ вүқ \argmin_{ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}} D_\textrm{KL}\big(ОҪ \| ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}\big).$$

~~~
As formulated, the operator version of REINFORCE computes the projection
perfectly in each step, while the REINFORCE performs just one step
of gradient descent in the direction of $рқ“ҹ$. However, it can be shown that
the fixed points of both algorithms are the same.

---
# Trajectory Formulation of OP-REINFORCE

The proposition is actually not difficult to prove, we just need to expand the
definitions.

~~~
Denoting $ОҪ$ the distribution over trajectories such that
$ОҪ(П„) вҲқ G(П„) ПҖ(П„)$, we get

~~~
$$D_\textrm{KL}\big(ОҪ \| ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}) = вҲ«_П„ ОҪ(П„) \log \frac{ОҪ(П„)}{ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}(П„)} \d П„.$$

~~~
Therefore, the gradient is
$$\frac{вҲӮ D_\textrm{KL}\big(ОҪ \| ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}\big)}{вҲӮ вҶ’Оё}
 = - вҲ«_П„ ОҪ(П„) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} \log ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}(П„) \d П„
 вҲқ - вҲ«_П„ ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}(П„) G(П„) вҲҮ_{вҶ’Оё} \log ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}(П„) \d П„.$$

~~~
For policy $ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё^*}$ being a fixed point of the REINFORCE algorithm, the last term
is zero; therefore, $ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё^*}$ is also the fixed point of $рқ“ҹвҲҳрқ“ҳ$.

~~~
On the other hand, if $ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё^*}$ is the fixed point of $рқ“ҹвҲҳрқ“ҳ$, the derivative
of the $D_\textrm{KL}\big(ОҪ \| ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё})$ is zero, and therefore it is also the
fixed point of the REINFORCE algorithm.

---

# State-Action Formulation of OP-REINFORCE

We can formulate the operator view also employing the action-value function $q$
and the on-policy distribution $Ој_ПҖ$; however, the policy improvement operator
needs to return not just a policy, but a joint distribution over the states
and actions.

~~~
The REINFORCE algorithm can be seen as performing one gradient step to minimize
the composition $рқ“ҹвҲҳрқ“ҳ$, where
$$(рқ“ҳ ПҖ)(s, a) \stackrel{\tiny\textrm{def}}{вҲқ} Ој_ПҖ(s) q_ПҖ(s, a) ПҖ(a | s),$$
~~~
$$рқ“ҹ ОҪ вүқ \argmin_{ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}} рқ”ј_{sвҲјОҪ(s)} \Big[D_\textrm{KL}\big(ОҪ(вӢ…|s) \| ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}(вӢ…|s)\big)\Big].$$

---
class: dbend
# State-Action Formulation of OP-REINFORCE

For completeness, we can explicitly express the joint distribution
$(рқ“ҳ ПҖ)(s, a)$ as a product of $(рқ“ҳ ПҖ)(s) вӢ… (рқ“ҳ ПҖ)(a|s)$, where
- the distribution over the states is
  $$(рқ“ҳ ПҖ)(s) вүқ \frac{Ој_ПҖ(s) v_ПҖ(s)}{вҲ‘_{s'} Ој_ПҖ(s') v_ПҖ(s')} = \frac{Ој_ПҖ(s) v_ПҖ(s)}{рқ”ј_{s'вҲјОј_ПҖ} [v_ПҖ(s')]},$$

~~~
- the conditional distribution over the actions is
  $$(рқ“ҳ ПҖ)(a|s)
    вүқ \frac{q_ПҖ(s, a) ПҖ(a|s)}{вҲ‘_{a'} q_ПҖ(s, a') ПҖ(a'|s)}
    = \frac{q_ПҖ(s, a) ПҖ(a|s)}{рқ”ј_{a'вҲјПҖ(s)}[q_ПҖ(s, a')]}
    = \frac{q_ПҖ(s, a) ПҖ(a|s)}{v_ПҖ(s)}.$$

---

However, it is not obvious if $ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё^*}$ is still a fixed point of $рқ“ҹвҲҳрқ“ҳ^k$.
~~~

# Higher Powers of the Returns

Let us reformulate the $рқ“ҳ^k$ to $рқ“ҳ^\frac{1}{Оұ}$ for $Оұ вүӨ 1$:

$$(рқ“ҳ^\frac{1}{Оұ} ПҖ)(П„) \stackrel{\tiny\textrm{def}}{вҲқ} G(П„)^\frac{1}{Оұ} ПҖ(П„).$$

~~~
We then define a projection operator $рқ“ҹ^Оұ$ using $Оұ$-divergence (also known as
RГ©nyi divergence of order $Оұ$) instead of the KL divergence:

$$\begin{aligned}
  рқ“ҹ^Оұ ОҪ & вүқ \argmin_{вҶ’Оё} D^Оұ\big(ОҪ \| ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё}\big),\\
  D^Оұ(p\|q) & вүқ \frac{1}{1 - Оұ} \log рқ”ј_{xвҲјp} \big[(p(x)/q(x))^{Оұ-1}\big] .\\
\end{aligned}$$

~~~
For $Оұ=1$, $D^Оұ$ is not defined, but its limit in $Оұ вҶ’ 1$ is $D_\mathrm{KL}$.

~~~
Proposition 8 of the OP-REINFORCE paper proves that for $Оұ вҲҲ (0,1)$, $ПҖ_{вҶ’Оё^*}$
is the fixed point of $рқ“ҹ^ОұвҲҳрқ“ҳ^\frac{1}{Оұ}$.

# Actor-Critic Methods

It is possible to combine the policy gradient methods and temporal difference
methods, creating a family of algorithms usually called the **actor-critic** methods.

~~~
The idea is straightforward: similarly to the REINFORCE with baseline, we train
the policy network together with the value network. However, instead of
estimating the episode return using the whole episode rewards, we use $n$-step
return TD estimate in both the policy gradient and the mean squared
value error $\overline{VE}$.

# Asynchronous Methods for Deep RL

The A3C was introduced in a 2016 paper from Volodymyr Mnih et al. (the same group as DQN)
_Asynchronous Methods for Deep Reinforcement Learning_,
https://arxiv.org/abs/1602.01783.

~~~
The authors propose an asynchronous framework, where multiple workers share one
neural network, each training using either an off-line or on-line RL algorithm.

~~~
They compare 1-step Q-learning, 1-step Sarsa, $n$-step Q-learning and A3C
(an _asynchronous advantage actor-critic_ method). For A3C, they compare
a version with and without LSTM.

~~~
The authors also introduce the **entropy regularization term**
$$-ОІ H(ПҖ(s; вҶ’Оё))$$
to the loss to support exploration and discourage premature convergence (they
use $ОІ=0.01$).

~~~
The entropy regularization has since become the standard way of encouraging
exploration with a policy network.
~~~
The entropy regularization keeps a controllable level of surprise (i.e.,
exploration) in the distribution.
~~~
Compared to $Оө$-greedy approach, the exploration actions are sampled
proportionally to their expected utility, not randomly.

# Asynchronous Methods for Deep RL

All methods perform updates every 5 actions
($t_\textrm{max}=I_\textrm{AsyncUpdate}=5$), updating the target
network each $40\,000$ frames.

~~~
The Atari inputs are processed as in DQN, using also action repeat 4.

~~~
The network architecture is: 16 filters $8Г—8$ stride 4, 32
filters $4Г—4$ stride 2, followed by a fully connected layer with 256 units.
All hidden layers apply a ReLU nonlinearity. Values and/or action values
are then generated from the (same) last hidden layer.

~~~
- The network is analogous to the DQN one, but with one less convolution
  layer and less convolution filters because both action prediction
  and training is performed on a CPU.

~~~
The LSTM methods utilize a 256-unit LSTM cell after the dense hidden layer.

~~~
All experiments use a discount factor of $Оі=0.99$ and use RMSProp with
momentum decay factor of $0.99$.

# Parallel Advantage Actor Critic

An alternative to independent workers is to train in a synchronous and
centralized way by having the workers to only generate episodes. Such approach

The authors use $8$ workers, $n_e=32$ parallel environments, $5$-step returns,
$Оі=0.99$, $Оө=0.1$, $ОІ=0.01$, and a learning rate of $Оұ=0.0007вӢ…n_e=0.0224$.

~~~
The $\textrm{arch}_\textrm{nips}$ is from A3C: 16 filters $8Г—8$ stride 4, 32
filters $4Г—4$ stride 2, a dense layer with 256 units. The
$\textrm{arch}_\textrm{nature}$ is from DQN: 32 filters $8Г—8$ stride 4, 64
filters $4Г—4$ stride 2, 64 filters $3Г—3$ stride 1, and 512-unit fully connected
layer. All nonlinearities are ReLU.
